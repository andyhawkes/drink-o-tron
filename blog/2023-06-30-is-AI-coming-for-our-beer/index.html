Is AI coming for our beer?

Apologies for the clickbait headline, but it's 2023 and the AI revolution is upon us (apparently), so what are you going to do?...

Dislaimer: this blog post is actually more of a "wow, AI is actually quite clever and fun, but it's not going to replace humans just yet" kind of thing.

Anyway, back to the point - I found myself wondering "how well would ChatGPT do at generating a name and a beer review for one of the random beer ideas that this site can generate?"

So here we go...

First we need a random beer...

Gluten-free salted caramel american-style double india pale ale Hops: Spalt, Cekin, Sun, Chinook & Willamette

OK, that's sufficiently weird and wonderful, so let's ask ChatGPT for a name...

Invent a name for a Gluten-free salted caramel american-style double india pale ale brewed with Spalt, Cekin, Sun, Chinook & Willamette hops

Caramel Hopsplash: A Gluten-Free Salty Temptation

OK - not too bad, as beer names go.
How about a review?

Write a short review of a Gluten-free salted caramel american-style double india pale ale brewed with Spalt, Cekin, Sun, Chinook & Willamette hops

The Gluten-Free Salted Caramel American-Style Double India Pale Ale is a unique and intriguing brew that pushes the boundaries of traditional beer flavors. This beer combines the popular American-style double IPA with the decadent notes of salted caramel, resulting in a rich and complex drinking experience.

OK, sounds plausible...

One notable feature of this beer is its gluten-free composition, which caters to those with gluten sensitivities or dietary restrictions. The fact that it manages to maintain its distinct flavors while being gluten-free is a commendable achievement. This makes it an excellent option for individuals looking to explore flavorful craft beers without compromising their dietary needs.

How very considerate!

Upon pouring, the beer showcases a beautiful amber hue with a slight haze, hinting at its hop-forward nature. The aroma is a delightful blend of hoppy goodness and sweet caramel notes. The combination of Spalt, Cekin, Sun, Chinook, and Willamette hops lends the brew a robust hop profile with hints of citrus, pine, and floral undertones.

I was hoping for "a delightful brew - hoppy, nutty, foamy, with a surprisingly fruity note which lingers on the tongue", but that's just because I like the movie "The World's End"...

The taste is where this beer truly shines. The initial sip reveals a burst of hop bitterness that quickly gives way to the sweetness of salted caramel. The caramel flavor is well-balanced, providing a luscious and indulgent quality without overpowering the hoppy character. The use of salted caramel adds a layer of complexity, enhancing the overall flavor profile and creating a harmonious interplay between the hops and sweetness.

OK, still worryingly plausible...

The mouthfeel is smooth and full-bodied, typical of a double IPA, with a moderate carbonation level that complements the beer's flavors. The finish is pleasantly bitter, leaving a lingering hoppy aftertaste with a subtle touch of caramel sweetness.

And at the end of the day, who doesn't love a bit of moderate carbonation?!?

Overall, the Gluten-Free Salted Caramel American-Style Double India Pale Ale is a unique and enjoyable beer that successfully combines the best of both worldsâ€”bold hoppy notes and the indulgent richness of salted caramel. Its gluten-free composition is a commendable feature, broadening its appeal to a wider audience. If you're a fan of hop-forward beers with a twist of sweetness, this brew is certainly worth a try.

So there you go - a quite reasonable-sounding review of a randomly made-up beer created by a chat bot built on top of a large language model.

OK, so maybe AI is going to steal our jobs, but not quite yet, and at least it can't drink our beer for us!

Maybe I should contemplate paying for access to the ChatGPT API and have it create a review for every random beer idea that this site generates. What do you think?
